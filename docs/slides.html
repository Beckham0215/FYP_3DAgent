01Problem Statement & Objectives

Digital twins are stunning — but passive and disconnected

13D twins are passive — manual click-by-click navigation, no understanding of what is in the space.

Why hybrid vision? A fixed 80-class detector can't name a centrifuge or a fire extinguisher. So an open-vocabulary LLM (Scout) names whatever it sees, then local CV localises exactly those names with tight boxes and outlines — accurate names and reliable boxes, with a local-CV fallback if the cloud is unavailable.

05Conclusion

Achievements, honest limitations, and what's next

✓ Achievements

Conversational agent over a live twin — 14 intents, voice + text
Hybrid open-vocab vision: detect, count & outline assets
Auto-tag whole floors from vision
Maintenance pinned in 3D with severity triage
ReAct planning with visual verification
Rigorous evaluation suite (CIs, slot-filling, latency)

⚠ Current Limitations

Vision/LLM need internet + Groq API; free-tier daily token cap
Scans take a few seconds (cloud + local pipeline)
Auto-tag is geometry-based, not wall-aware (doorway edge-cases)
Single-user ownership; no multi-tenant org accounts yet

→ Future Work

On-device / cached models to cut internet dependence
Wall-aware room segmentation for perfect auto-tagging
Scheduled re-scans + inventory-drift alerts
Mobile app + QR deep-links from asset → twin & history
Multi-tenant org accounts & collaboration
